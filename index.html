Hello there.

My name is Michelle, twenty-seven, and an aspiring software developer, economist, and researcher. NJ-born, MNL-raised resident of Miami, FL. Just your average pantomath-aspiring philomath-polymath.

About Me

I am an accounting and administrative assistant for Moon Thai Group, the umbrella company that owns Moon Thai & Japanese and Ohho in Florida. Previously, I was a content and financial analyst and, very briefly, an economic researcher. Possessed with an inherently inquisitive, highly-imaginative mind, I would always find myself engaged in the pursuit of knowledge and over the moon in mental stimulation. I delved into social sciences and aspired to be a researcher.

To most, it was an aspiration that was very one-sided in a left-brain kind of way. Ruled by logic and methodology, there was no room for creativity. I begged to differ; it wasn't a science-and-church-separation kind of way.

I was fascinated by the idea of the expression of creativity through the application of logic. It’s really not at all impossible. In fact, this was how I stumbled upon software development. After all, haven’t they always been two sides of the same coin that is software development? Or better yet, its essence?

And before I knew it, I have found my niche: to be a software developer. To engage in the creation of meaning through the marriage of logic and creativity. I hope to continue to grow as a developer and to learn from great programmers, whom I hope to have the chance to work alongside with.

Outside of my professional life, I am also a bartender and an occasional mixologist, bibliophile, music-lover-worshipper, aspiring photographer*, and all-around nerd.
